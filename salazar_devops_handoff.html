Insurance-Support Workflow
Full claim lifecycle — homeowner-owned, contractor-documented
⚖ Texas compliance — critical framing

The Texas Department of Insurance prohibits a roofing contractor from acting as a public adjuster on a claim for a property where they may also do the work. Salazar may provide contractor estimates, photos, and technical information. The homeowner owns and manages the insurance claim.

All app UI language must reflect: "Insurance-support documentation" — NOT "claim filing," "claim negotiation," or "claim settlement."

Customer Portal & Notification Engine
Magic link portal, 13 automated milestones, SMS + email delivery

Portal access model

No app download. No password. Office or automation generates a portal_token UUID, attached to the job and contact. Token is embedded in a short-link SMS. When homeowner taps the link, they see their branded job tracker. Token rotates on each new milestone notification. Expiry default: 180 days.

What the homeowner sees in the portal

  • Current job status with visual stage progress
  • Next step description
  • Upcoming appointment dates
  • Job timeline with completed / active / pending markers
  • Photos shared by Salazar (is_customer_visible = true)
  • Documents: contract, estimate, invoice, warranty
  • Payment link (Stripe checkout)
  • Message thread (read + optional reply)
  • Insurance claim status (if applicable)
  • Change order approvals (remodeling)
  • Final walkthrough checklist
  • Referral link + QR code + reward status
  • Review request (after warranty upload)

Ethical canvassing guardrails

Required in every canvassing mission
  • Digital ID badge displayed on canvasser mobile screen
  • Company license + insurance info accessible
  • Do-not-knock address check before every door is shown as available
  • No-soliciting flag shown if flagged by prior rep
  • Consent checkbox before entering homeowner phone/email
  • Right-to-cancel notice if any verbal agreement is made at the door (Texas 3-business-day rule)
  • Approved script loaded — no ad-lib insurance language
  • No deductible waiver offers under any circumstances

Existing-customer storm check-in

When storm_customer_impacts records are created, office can launch a check-in campaign to opted-in past customers. Approved template only:

"Salazar Roofing & Remodeling: We saw severe weather reported near your area. We hope your home is safe. Reply INSPECT if you'd like us to schedule a no-pressure roof check. Reply STOP to opt out."

Texas insurance law — key requirements

RuleRequirementApp enforcement
TDI — public adjuster prohibitionContractor cannot act as public adjuster on claim where they also do the workAll UI copy uses "insurance-support documentation" language. Blocked phrases list enforced in template editor.
TDI — deductible waiver prohibitionContractors cannot waive, rebate, or absorb homeowner deductiblesDeductible field is display-only in portal. Warning banner on all insurance jobs. Blocked phrase list.
Texas insurance code — contract disclosureContracts involving insurance proceeds must include deductible payment noticeContract template includes required TDI disclosure. Admin cannot publish template without approval flag.
Right to cancel — door-to-door sales3 business days to cancel contracts signed at the homeCanvasser app generates right-to-cancel notice PDF on any in-field agreement. Required before lead can advance.

SMS / TCPA requirements

RuleRequirementApp enforcement
A2P 10DLC registrationAll business SMS via Twilio must be registered with TCRAdmin setup checklist includes 10DLC registration step. Campaigns blocked until registered.
Marketing consentAutodialed/prerecorded marketing SMS requires prior express written consentsms_consents.consent_type = marketing required before campaign sends. UI blocks send if missing.
STOP handlingMust honor opt-outs immediatelyTwilio STOP webhook → opt_outs record + sms_consents.revoked_at. All subsequent sends blocked.
Message identificationBusiness name must appear in messageAll templates include "Salazar Roofing & Remodeling:" prefix. Template editor enforces.

Referral & review compliance

  • Referral rewards must never be used to reduce or cover an insurance deductible
  • Rewards paid only after referred job is complete and paid in full
  • Review requests must not require a positive review as a condition of any reward
  • Any material connection between a reward and a review must be disclosed in the review
  • Loyalty program must be documented and applied consistently to avoid unfair competition claims
